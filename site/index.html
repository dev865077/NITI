Research map

One scalar, three execution surfaces.

NITI keeps the central claim narrow: the oracle scalar that settles a DLC can also activate prepared contract edges. The same witness can be studied on-chain, inside channel conditions, and inside proof models.

01

Bitcoin cDLCs

A parent DLC outcome funds a bridge transaction. The bridge creates the funding output of a prepared child DLC.

02

Lightning extension

The same attestation scalar becomes a channel witness: an HTLC preimage today, a point-lock scalar when PTLCs are available.

03

Machine checking

Ada/SPARK models prove the finite algebra for adaptor completion, wrong-secret rejection, and Lightning witness behavior.

Limitations

Precise claims are more useful than broad claims.

NITI proves and tests an activation primitive. A production system still needs protocol engineering, operational hardening, liquidity, collateral design, and external review.

Not a new consensus rule

Bitcoin validates ordinary signatures and timelocks; meaning stays off-chain.

Not a full Lightning implementation

The LND harness prepares HTLC tests. It does not prove force-close or routing behavior.

Not an economic guarantee

Stable exposure still requires collateral, liquidation policy, liquidity, and oracle governance.
